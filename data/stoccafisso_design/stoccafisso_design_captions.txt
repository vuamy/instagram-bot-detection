#MakingThings 🪁How do you automate a miniature Viking warrior’s hair blowing in an imaginary breeze? How do you build a wooden hedgehog and ferret so they clap their hands in sync? How do you make a thimble-sized ice skater pirouette? These are just a few recent challenges @stoccafisso_design (Amedeo Capelli) has set for himself in his home garage “laboratory.” Amedeo, an inventor and sculptor who creates mechanical toys — or “automatons” — full of artistic details and wonder, taught himself this craft last summer after craving a new project. “I love popular folklore and mysterious stories, so my characters follow various universes, from fairytale to fantasy,” he says. “And I like to depict the elements of nature, such as the wind making a kite fly.” Amedeo says inspiration for his kinetic sculptures can strike at any time – especially when observing the gestures of people or animals in his rural Italian village. Each piece takes between one and four days to complete; nearly 300 small automatons are already under Amedeo’s belt. Larger-scale creations and more complex scenes are coming soon, he says. “I always have so much more in my mind.” 💭

This automaton is inspired by a sad true story, a mystery that has lasted for more than 70 years, which has now become part of British folklore. It is a macabre and intriguing story, which has always fascinated me, so I decided to dedicate one of my works to it.

It was April 18, 1943, near Stourbridge, on the hill of Wychbury Hill, four boys intent on practicing birdnesting, came across an old elm, with the usual form dense with branches and hollows in the stem. They decided to climb on it, suddenly made a sensational discovery, inside a deep hollow there were the remains of a skeleton, at first they thought of animal remains, but analyzing them better revealed human remains. The body belonged to a woman around 35 years of age who had been deceased for at least 18 months. All the bones of her body were present except for one hand, which will be found in the same wood. It presented objects, including a shoe, a gold ring and pieces of clothing. The body will never be identified and she will be given the name Bella. I will not dwell now, since this is not the place, on the conduct of the investigations, also because what interests me is the folkloristic part of the story, which, like all unresolved crime reports, acquires paranormal, magical and ritualistic shades, given that there are those who hypothesized that it was a murder for ritual purposes, an indication of this is the lack of the hand, hypothetically used for the rite of the "hand of glory". An interesting development of the story occurs when six months later the written on a wall "Who put Bella in the wich elm?" It is not known who the author of the graffiti is, but the fact is that these begin to spread, even changing to "Who put Bella in the witch elm?" Is this a bad joke or someone intending to communicate something to the detectives? The fact is that this spread of writings makes the story definitively enter the left imagination of the area. #tree #skeleton #automata #automaton #creepy #creepyart #creepycute #truecrime #folktale #ukfolk #folklegend #halloween #weird #weirdtales #humanskull #skeletoninthetree
